Why was Rosa Parks Known?

Rosa Parks was a civil rights activist. She was known for her pivotal role in the Montgomery bus boycott. The US Congress has called her "the first lady of civil rights" and "the mother of the freedom movement". Rosa Parks' act of defiance and the Montgomery bus boycott had become important symbols of the movement. She became an international icon of resistance to racial segregation.

Timeline of Rosa Parks

  • February 4, 1913 The day Rosa Parks was born.
  • 1929 Parks left school to take care of her sick grandmother and mother.
  • 1932 She married her Raymond Parks.
  • 1933 She earned her highschool degree.
  • December 1, 1955 Parks was arrested for refusing a bus driver's instructions to give up her seat to a white passenger.
  • October 24, 2005 Rosa Parks died of progressive dementia, which she had been suffering from since at least 2002.
